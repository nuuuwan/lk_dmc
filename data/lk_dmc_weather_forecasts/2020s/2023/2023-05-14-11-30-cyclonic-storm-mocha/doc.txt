Warning for extremely severe cyclonic storm “Mocha” over East-Central Bay of Bengal Issued by the Natural Hazards Early Warning Centre Issued at 11:30 a.m. 14 May 2023, valid for 11:30 a.m. 15 May 2023

For Multi-day boats in the Bay of Bengal Sea area. PLEASE BE AWARE! Extremely severe cyclonic storm “Mocha” over East-Central Bay of Bengal lay centered near 18.7N and 91.5E at 05.30 a.m. today (14th May). It is very likely to move north-northeastwards and cross southeast Bangladesh and north Myanmar coasts around noon of 14th May.

Expected path of the cyclone (Source: RSMC-India)

Due to this system, wind speeds will be (55-65) kmph and can increase up to (75-85) kmph in sea areas bounded by (03N - 18N) and (78E – 85E) and wind speeds will be (180-190) kmph and can increase up to 210 kmph in sea areas bounded by (10N - 23N) and (86E – 100E) during next 24 hours.

Heavy showers or thundershowers; very rough, high, or very high seas at times can also be expected in above sea areas.
