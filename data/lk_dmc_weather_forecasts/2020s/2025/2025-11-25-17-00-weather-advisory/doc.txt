Bulletin No: 04 MSA/25/11/25/01 COLOR: Amber Advisory for strong winds, heavy showers and rough seas.

Issued by the Natural Hazards Early Warning Centre Issued at 5.00 p.m. 25 November 2025, valid for the next 24 hours.

For Multi-day boats in the Bay of Bengal deep sea area, and for fishing and naval

communities engaging in activities in the sea areas around the island.

PLEASE BE AWARE! 1. For Multi-day boats in the Bay of Bengal deep sea area

The low-pressure area over South Andaman Sea area moved west-northwestwards, intensified into a Depression, and lay over Strait of Malacca. It is likely to move westwards and intensify further during next 24 hours.

The wind speed in the sea areas marked under the “Advisory” category on the map below will increase to (50-60) kmph at times with heavy thundershowers and those sea areas will be rough at times.

Wind speeds in the sea areas marked under the “Warning” category may increase to 70 kmph at times, heavy thundershowers can occur, and the sea conditions in these areas will become very rough. Naval and fishing communities are warned not to venture to these sea areas until further notice. Naval and fishing communities operating in those sea areas are advised to move away from the affected sea areas.

2. For fishing and naval communities engaging in activities in the sea areas around the

island.

The low-level atmospheric disturbance over the southwest Bay of Bengal and Sri Lanka has intensified into a low-pressure area by today morning (November 25). It is likely to intensify further during the next 24 hours.

The sea areas around the island will be rough at times as the wind speed can be increased up to 60 kmph.

Navel and fishing communities are requested to be attentive to the future forecasts and bulletins issued by the Department of Meteorology in this regard
