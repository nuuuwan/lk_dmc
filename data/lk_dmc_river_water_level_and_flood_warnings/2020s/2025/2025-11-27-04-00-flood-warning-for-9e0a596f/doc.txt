Due to the significant rainfall continuously received over the upper and middle catchment areas of the Deduru Oya Basin during the past 24 hours, and the ongoing release of water through the spill gates of the Deduru Oya Reservoir at a discharge rate of approximately 33,000 cubic feet per second into the Deduru Oya, it is hereby warned that the prevailing flood situation in the lower valley areas of the Deduru Oya Basin is likely to intensify up to a major flood level. According to the observations of the Irrigation Department’s gauging stations, this critical flood risk applies to the areas belonging to the Divisional Secretariat Divisions of Wariyapola, Nikaweratiya, Mahawa, Kobeigane, Bingiriya, Pallama, Chilaw, Arachchikattuwa and Rasnayakapura. Furthermore, the flood warning previously issued under my reference number DI/Hydro/103(V) through Flood Warning Bulletin No. 03 for the Deduru Oya Basin at 9.00 p.m. on 26 November 2025 is hereby extended for a further period of 48 hours. The general public residing in these areas, as well as vehicle drivers travelling through the affected routes, are kindly requested to act with extreme caution and to remain highly vigilant in view of this situation. Relevant disaster management authorities are also requested to take all necessary measures in this regard.
